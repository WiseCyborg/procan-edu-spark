## Your Role & Responsibilities

### What is a Training Coordinator?

As a Training Coordinator, you are the bridge between your Dispensary Manager and your employees. You're responsible for ensuring that all staff members complete their required training and maintain compliance with Maryland cannabis regulations.

### Key Responsibilities

- **Employee Onboarding**: Invite new employees and assign training seats
- **Progress Monitoring**: Track employee progress through the 18-module course
- **Engagement**: Send reminders and provide support to keep employees on track
- **Reporting**: Generate compliance reports for management and audits
- **Communication**: Serve as the first point of contact for training-related questions

### Relationship to Dispensary Manager

- Your manager purchases training seats and oversees the organization
- You manage day-to-day training operations and employee progress
- You report training metrics and compliance status to your manager
- You can request additional seats when needed

## Managing Employees

### How to Invite Employees (2 Methods)

#### Method 1: Email Invitation (Recommended)
1. Navigate to the "Invite Employees" tab
2. Enter the employee's email address
3. Click "Send Invitation"
4. The employee receives an email with registration instructions
5. Once they register, a seat is automatically assigned

#### Method 2: Share Access Key
1. Your manager provides you with the organization's access key
2. Share this key directly with employees
3. Employees enter the key during registration
4. A seat is automatically assigned upon registration

### Assigning Seats to Employees

Seats are typically assigned automatically during registration. However, you can manually assign seats when needed:

1. Go to "Employee Management" tab
2. Click "Manage Seats" button
3. View available seats and unassigned employees
4. Select an employee and click "Assign Seat"
5. Confirmation will appear once the seat is assigned

### Monitoring Progress

**Progress Indicators:**
- **0-33%**: Red indicator - Employee needs immediate attention
- **34-66%**: Yellow indicator - Employee is making progress
- **67-100%**: Green indicator - Employee is on track

**Tier Achievements:**
- **Green Tier**: Modules 0-5 (Foundational knowledge)
- **Yellow Tier**: Modules 6-11 (Intermediate compliance)
- **Red Tier**: Modules 12-17 (Advanced operations)

**Dashboard Views:**
- Click any employee's name to see detailed module-by-module progress
- View time spent on each module
- See quiz scores and completion dates
- Identify which modules are challenging

### Understanding Tier Achievements

The course uses a color-coded tier system:

- **Green Tier (Foundation)**: Basic cannabis knowledge, legal framework, product types
- **Yellow Tier (Compliance)**: Security, inventory management, customer verification
- **Red Tier (Advanced)**: Crisis management, METRC, advanced compliance

Employees must complete all modules within a tier before progressing to the next tier.

### Setting Deadlines and Reminders

While your manager sets organization-wide deadlines, you can:

1. Send individual reminders to employees who are falling behind
2. Encourage employees approaching deadlines
3. Celebrate employees who complete tiers
4. Escalate concerns about employees who are unresponsive

## Seat Management

### Understanding Seat Types

**Available Seats**
- Purchased but not yet assigned to any employee
- Ready to be allocated to new hires
- Shown in green on your dashboard

**Assigned Seats**
- Allocated to a specific employee
- Employee can log in and access training
- Shown in blue on your dashboard

**Used Seats**
- Employee has started or completed training
- Cannot be reassigned to another employee
- Shown in gray on your dashboard

### Allocating Seats Efficiently

**Best Practices:**
- Assign seats immediately after hiring decisions
- Don't pre-assign seats until you have a confirmed start date
- Monitor seat utilization weekly
- Request more seats when you have < 5 available

**Tracking Seat Utilization:**
```
Utilization Rate = (Used Seats / Total Purchased Seats) × 100%
```
- Target: 80-90% utilization
- Below 60%: You may have purchased too many seats
- Above 95%: Request more seats from your manager

### Requesting More Seats from Manager

When you need additional seats:

1. Check current available seat count
2. Calculate how many seats you need (factor in upcoming hires)
3. Send a message to your Dispensary Manager via the Communication Hub
4. Include: current utilization rate, number of pending hires, requested quantity
5. Manager will purchase seats and notify you when they're available

## Monitoring & Reporting

### Reading Progress Dashboards

**Overview Metrics:**
- **Total Employees**: All staff members in the system
- **Average Progress**: Mean completion percentage across all employees
- **At-Risk Students**: Employees < 50% complete or near deadline
- **Completion Rate**: Percentage of employees who've finished all 18 modules

**Individual Employee Cards:**
- Progress bar showing overall completion
- Current tier (Green/Yellow/Red)
- Last activity timestamp
- Certificate status

### Identifying At-Risk Employees

An employee is considered "at-risk" if:
- Progress < 50% with < 14 days until deadline
- No activity in the past 7 days
- Failed the final exam twice
- Stuck on the same module for > 5 days

**Action Steps for At-Risk Employees:**
1. Send a reminder email via dashboard
2. Reach out personally via phone or text
3. Offer to schedule a help session
4. Escalate to manager if unresponsive after 3 attempts

### Generating Compliance Reports

**Types of Reports:**

1. **Progress Report**
   - Employee name, progress %, current tier, last activity
   - Download as CSV for spreadsheet analysis
   - Use for weekly manager updates

2. **Certificate Report**
   - Employee name, certificate number, issue date, expiry date
   - Download for compliance audits
   - Verify all employees have valid certificates

3. **Seat Utilization Report**
   - Total purchased, available, assigned, used
   - Utilization percentage
   - Use for budget planning

**How to Generate:**
1. Go to "Analytics & Reports" tab
2. Select report type
3. Choose date range (if applicable)
4. Click "Download Report"
5. File downloads as CSV

### Exporting Data for Audits

During regulatory audits, you may need to provide:
- Complete training records for all employees
- Certificate verification
- Proof of completion dates
- Quiz scores and time spent

**Audit Preparation Checklist:**
- [ ] Download Certificate Report
- [ ] Download Progress Report  
- [ ] Verify all employees have valid certificates
- [ ] Ensure all completion dates are accurate
- [ ] Have organization access key ready
- [ ] Print hard copies if required by inspector

## Communication

### Sending Reminders to Employees

**When to Send Reminders:**
- Employee hasn't started training within 3 days of registration
- Employee inactive for 7+ days
- Employee < 50% complete with < 14 days until deadline
- Employee failed final exam (encouraging message)

**How to Send Reminders:**
1. Navigate to "Employee Management" tab
2. Find the employee in the list
3. Click "Send Reminder" button
4. Message is automatically sent via email

**Reminder Best Practices:**
- Be encouraging, not punitive
- Include specific next steps ("Complete Module 5")
- Mention deadline if urgent
- Offer to help if they're stuck

### Using the Messaging System

The Communication Hub allows direct messaging with employees and your manager.

**To Send a Message:**
1. Click "Messages" in the top navigation
2. Click "New Conversation"
3. Select recipient (employee or manager)
4. Type your message
5. Click "Send"

**Message Types:**
- **Quick Check-In**: "How's training going?"
- **Support Offer**: "I noticed you're on Module 8. Need any help?"
- **Celebration**: "Congrats on completing Green Tier!"
- **Escalation**: "Can we schedule a call to discuss your progress?"

### Best Practices for Engagement

**Weekly Routine:**
- Monday: Review weekend progress, send reminders to inactive employees
- Wednesday: Check in with at-risk employees
- Friday: Send encouragement to employees who made progress

**Monthly Routine:**
- Generate progress report for manager
- Review seat utilization
- Celebrate employees who completed tiers
- Plan for upcoming training needs

**Tone Guidelines:**
- Professional but friendly
- Encouraging and supportive
- Clear and concise
- Action-oriented

### Maintaining High Completion Rates

**Strategies:**
- Set clear expectations upfront
- Check in regularly (but don't micromanage)
- Celebrate milestones (tier completions)
- Address issues early before they become problems
- Create friendly competition (e.g., "First to complete Red Tier wins lunch!")

**Efficient Coordinator Schedule:**
- **Daily (10 minutes)**: Quick dashboard check, respond to urgent messages
- **Weekly (30 minutes)**: Review at-risk list, send reminders, generate progress report
- **Monthly (1 hour)**: Deep dive into analytics, plan for next month, meet with manager

### Building a Training Culture

- Make training a priority, not an afterthought
- Lead by example (complete your own training promptly)
- Share success stories ("Sarah completed all 18 modules in 2 weeks!")
- Integrate training discussions into team meetings
- Recognize and reward completion

## Troubleshooting

### Common Employee Issues and Solutions

**Issue: Employee didn't receive invitation email**
- **Solution**: Check spam folder, verify email address, resend invitation

**Issue: Employee can't log in**
- **Solution**: Verify they completed registration, check if account is active, reset password via "Forgot Password"

**Issue: Employee says they don't have a seat**
- **Solution**: Check seat allocation, manually assign if needed, verify organization has available seats

**Issue: Employee stuck on a module**
- **Solution**: Encourage them to review module content again, watch video if available, take quiz multiple times for practice

**Issue: Employee failed final exam**
- **Solution**: Encourage them to review modules, especially Red Tier, wait 24 hours before retaking, offer to discuss challenging topics

**Issue: Progress not updating**
- **Solution**: Have employee log out and log back in, clear browser cache, try different browser

**Issue: Certificate not generating**
- **Solution**: Verify all 18 modules completed + final exam passed, check email spam folder, contact support if issue persists

### Contact Information for Support

**Technical Issues:**
- Email: support@procannedu.com
- Response time: Within 24 hours (usually faster)
- Include: Employee name, issue description, screenshots if possible

**Billing/Seat Questions:**
- Contact your Dispensary Manager first
- Manager can escalate to ProCann Edu if needed

**Content Questions:**
- Review FAQ page: [Link to FAQ]
- Check Training Handbook: [Link to Handbook]
- Email support for specific content questions

### Escalation Procedures

**Level 1 - Handle Yourself:**
- Minor technical issues (login, password reset)
- Reminder emails
- Progress check-ins

**Level 2 - Notify Manager:**
- Employee unresponsive after 3 attempts
- Employee refusing to complete training
- Seat shortage impacting operations
- Unusual technical issues affecting multiple employees

**Level 3 - Contact Support:**
- System-wide technical problems
- Billing discrepancies
- Certificate generation failures
- Data integrity concerns

## Appendix: Glossary of Terms

**Access Key**: Unique code used by employees to register and link to your organization

**At-Risk Employee**: Student with < 50% progress or approaching deadline without adequate progress

**Certificate**: Official document issued upon successful completion of all 18 modules and final exam

**Module**: Individual training lesson (18 total in the course)

**Progress Percentage**: Overall completion metric calculated from completed modules and activities

**Seat**: Training license that allows one employee to access the course

**Tier**: Color-coded grouping of modules (Green = 0-5, Yellow = 6-11, Red = 12-17)

**Utilization Rate**: Percentage of purchased seats that have been assigned/used
